Wellness Recovery Action Plan

The Wellness Recovery Action Plan (WRAP) is a personalized plan that helps individuals maintain their mental health and wellness.

Here are the main points of a WRAP:

Wellness Toolbox: A list of skills and strategies to maintain wellness and get back on track if needed. These tools can include activities like exercise, creative endeavors, and relaxation techniques.

Daily Maintenance Plan: A daily structure incorporating wellness tools. It describes how you look and feel when you're well, daily activities to maintain wellness, and activities to support overall wellbeing.

Triggers and Action Plan: Identifies external events that might cause distress and outlines appropriate responses.

Early Warning Signs and Action Plan: Recognizes subtle signs that things might be going off track and provides a plan to address them before they become major issues.

Crisis Plan: Establishes a plan for when things are breaking down, detailing who to contact, information to provide, and steps supporters can take to help you through a crisis.

Post-Crisis Plan: A guide for recovery after a crisis, reflecting on what was learned and how to move forward.

Developing a WRAP involves identifying personal resources, building a strong support system, and committing to an ongoing process of self-awareness and improvement.
Meanwhile, the NHS provides comprehensive information on treatment options, coping strategies, and where to seek urgent help. Additionally, websites like Bipolar UK offer forums and support groups where you can connect with others who understand what you're going through.
